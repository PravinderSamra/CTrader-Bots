## PART 1: CARMINE ROSATO'S ORDER FLOW STRATEGY

### Background and Philosophy
Carmine Rosato is a professional trader and educator whose methodology is built around reading markets as "live, continuous auctions." His core insight is that price constantly moves in search of liquidity, and that large institutional participants leave identifiable footprints in the order flow data. Rather than relying on lagging indicators or predictive tools, he trains traders to read what is actually happening in real time: who is buying, who is selling, which side is being aggressive, and whether momentum is building or fading.

His published playbooks focus on two primary setups: the **Low Volume Node (LVN) setup** and the **Real Fair Value Gap (FVG) setup**, both grounded in auction market theory.

---

### Carmine's Three Primary Order Flow Signals

1. **Absorption** — When aggressive orders hit the market but price barely moves, the opposing side is strong enough to absorb all that pressure. This reveals potential turning points and trapped traders. A long wick on a candle with high volume but no follow-through is classic absorption.

2. **Liquidity Walls** — Large clusters of limit orders visible on the DOM/order book that act as temporary barriers where price tends to stall, slow, or reverse before reacting.

3. **Stop Runs** — The market pushes through obvious highs or lows to trigger retail stop losses before reversing. This is a liquidity-grabbing mechanism used by larger players to fill their own orders at better prices.

---

### Setup 1: The Low Volume Node (LVN) Strategy

**Markets:** Futures and Indices (ES, NQ, DAX, Nasdaq) — intraday scalping/day trading.

**Timeframe:** First 2–3 hours of the trading session when institutional participation is highest. Avoid the midday lull (12:00–2:00 PM EST).

**Step-by-Step Setup Criteria:**

1. **Identify a Level of Interest** — Mark a clear supply zone, demand zone, support or resistance level. The level must show prior consolidation followed by an impulsive move away, confirming strong buyers or sellers were active.

2. **Wait for Price to Move Away** — Allow price to leave the level decisively. This validates the level as significant.

3. **Identify the Low Volume Node** — Use a volume-by-price profile to locate the area where price moved rapidly through with minimal volume. LVNs form during breakouts/breakdowns when price moved quickly — these are thin areas in market structure.

4. **Wait for Price to Return** — Monitor for the market to return toward the LVN zone.

5. **Seek Order Flow Confirmation** — Apply one or more tools to verify large buyers or sellers are defending the level:
   - **Heatmap (Bookmap):** Watch for iceberg orders or large limit order clusters at the LVN.
   - **Footprint chart / delta:** Look for absorption or delta divergence.
   - **DOM:** Watch for large passive bids or offers appearing and being defended.
   - **Cumulative Delta:** Watch for CVD to hold firm or reverse even as price dips into the zone.

**Entry:** Long on buyer defense; short on seller defense at the LVN.

**Stop Loss:** Just beyond the LVN zone, or beyond the recent swing high/low defining the level.

**Profit Targets:**
- High/Low of day
- Next supply or demand zone
- Another LVN above or below
- Prior session highs/lows or structural resistance/support
- Scale out: partial profit at first target, remainder runs

**Tools Required:** Volume profile, heatmap (Bookmap), footprint charts, delta/CVD

---

### Setup 2: The Real Fair Value Gap (FVG) Setup

A **real FVG** forms when large market participants transact at market prices causing price to move rapidly, leaving a gap between candle wicks with no trading in between. Carmine distinguishes "real" FVGs (left by genuine institutional activity) from "fake" FVGs (random price noise).

**Identification Criteria for a Real FVG:**
- The gap must form following a clear displacement move (strong, impulsive directional candle)
- Volume during displacement must be significantly above average
- A BOS (Break of Structure) should accompany or follow
- The FVG should sit within a premium or discount zone aligned with higher-timeframe bias

**Entry:** Wait for price to retrace into the FVG zone. Enter on confirmation of rejection — bullish/bearish candlestick pattern, absorption on footprint, or CVD shift. Some traders place a limit order at the FVG midpoint.

**Stop Loss:** Beyond the outer boundary of the FVG.

**Target:** Origin of the impulsive move, next structure level, or daily high/low.

---

### Carmine's Three-Tier Confirmation Framework
**Context → Location → Confirmation**

- **Context:** Higher timeframe bias (daily/weekly trend, value area positioning, session structure)
- **Location:** Price has arrived at a specific level of interest (LVN, FVG, order block, session high/low)
- **Confirmation:** Real-time order flow signals at that location confirm buyer or seller defense

All three must align before entering a trade.

|------|---------|

## PART 2: OTHER TOP ORDER FLOW TRADERS

### Tom Dante — Swing Failure Pattern (SFP)

**Background:** London-based professional trader, 25+ years in futures, forex, crypto. Methodology focuses on liquidity identification and the Swing Failure Pattern.

**Core Philosophy:** The market is driven by liquidity. Retail traders' stop losses and pending orders create fuel that institutions exploit. Trading with institutions means anticipating where retail stops are, letting price sweep them, then entering in the opposite direction.

**The Swing Failure Pattern (SFP) — Complete Rules:**

An SFP is a false break of a prior swing high or low. Price violates the level, triggers stops, then closes back inside the prior range.

- **Bearish SFP:** Price exceeds a prior swing high, then closes below it within the same candle. Traps breakout buyers; then reverses.
- **Bullish SFP:** Price falls below a prior swing low, closes back above it. Traps breakout sellers; then reverses.

**Best Timeframes:** Daily and 4-hour SFPs carry the most weight. 1-hour is acceptable. Below that is too noisy.

**Entry:** As the candle closes back inside the range, or on retest of the swept level.

**Stop Loss:** Just beyond the extreme wick that created the sweep.

**Profit Targets:**
- First: Opposite side of the range
- Second: POC of volume profile, or next significant structural level
- Risk-reward typically 1:3 or better

**Highest Quality SFP Conditions:**
- Prior swing is obvious and clean (well-respected by multiple candles)
- Lots of stops resting at the level (multiple prior tests, ATH/ATL, round numbers)
- The sweep is quick and violent (not a slow grind)
- Volume confirms the sweep (spike during the wick)
- Combine with RSI divergence, Fibonacci levels (0.618–1.0), or volume profile POC

### ICT (Inner Circle Trader — Michael Huddleston) — Complete Methodology

**Background:** Most comprehensive and influential retail methodology of the past decade. Centered on the premise that markets are driven by an Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm (IPDA) designed to seek liquidity.

---

#### Market Structure

**Break of Structure (BOS):**
- Uptrend: price closes above the most recent significant swing high → trend continuation
- Downtrend: price closes below the most recent significant swing low → trend continuation

**Change of Character (CHoCH):**
- Uptrend: price breaks below the most recent swing low created by the last BOS → reversal warning
- Downtrend: price breaks above the most recent swing high created by the last BOS → reversal warning
- A valid CHoCH must break the swing created by the most recent BOS

**Market Structure Shift (MSS):**
- More aggressive than CHoCH: displacement + momentum through a swing high/low
- Higher probability reversal signal

---

#### Order Blocks

**Definition:** The last opposing candle before a strong impulsive move that caused a BOS. Represents where institutions accumulated/distributed before the big move.

- **Bullish OB:** Last bearish candle before a strong bullish impulse. Acts as support on return.
- **Bearish OB:** Last bullish candle before a strong bearish impulse. Acts as resistance on return.

**Validity Criteria:**
1. Followed by displacement (violent, impulsive move with momentum)
2. That displacement caused a BOS or liquidity sweep
3. OB is unmitigated (price has not yet returned to it)
4. Aligned with higher-timeframe bias
5. A liquidity grab immediately before the OB's displacement adds significant quality

**Entry:** Wait for price to return to the OB zone. Confirm with bullish/bearish candlestick pattern, rejection wick, or CHoCH on lower timeframe. Stop beyond OB extreme.

**OTE (Optimal Trade Entry):** The 62%–79% Fibonacci retracement of the OB range — ICT's preferred entry level.

---

#### Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

**Definition:** Three-candle pattern where there is a gap between the wick of candle 1 and wick of candle 3, with the middle candle having no overlap with either. Price moved too quickly for efficient two-way trading.

**Entry Methods:**
1. Limit order at the FVG midpoint (50%)
2. Wait for price to return and enter on rejection confirmation
3. Aggressive: enter on first touch of FVG boundary
4. Conservative: wait for CHoCH on lower timeframe after FVG is touched

**Quality Criteria:**
- Formed during a displacement move (not consolidation)
- Accompanied by a BOS or liquidity sweep
- Located in a premium/discount zone aligned with higher-timeframe bias
- Unmitigated

**Inverse FVG (IFVG):** When price fully closes through an FVG without reaction, it flips — becomes resistance (for bullish FVGs) or support (for bearish FVGs).

---

#### Liquidity Pools

- **Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL):** Above swing highs, equal highs, ATH, round numbers, previous session highs.
- **Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL):** Below swing lows, equal lows, ATL, round numbers, previous session lows.

**ICT Logic:** Institutions engineer price to sweep liquidity pools, triggering stops (which become their fill), then reverse in the intended direction. Wait for the sweep and reversal.

- **Discount Zone (below 50%):** Optimal for buying — look for bullish OBs, FVGs, liquidity grabs
- **Premium Zone (above 50%):** Optimal for selling — look for bearish OBs, FVGs, liquidity grabs
- **OTE:** 62%–79% Fibonacci retracement — ICT's preferred entry level

## PART 3: CORE ORDER FLOW CONCEPTS

### 3.1 Order Blocks

**What:** Zone where a large institution placed significant orders before a directional move. The last opposing candle before a strong impulse.

**Why:** Institutions accumulate in layers. When price returns to an OB, remaining unfilled orders create magnetic attraction. Price often reacts sharply on the first touch.

**How to identify:**
- Find a strong directional move that caused a BOS or swept a liquidity pool
- Go back to the candle immediately before that impulse began
- That candle is the order block — mark its high and low as the OB zone
- Valid until mitigated (price returns to it)

**Validity markers:** Preceded/followed by a liquidity grab; strong impulse afterward; aligned with higher TF; first touch is highest probability.

---

### 3.2 Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) / Imbalances

**What:** Three-candle pattern — candle 1 high and candle 3 low do not overlap (for bullish FVG), leaving a gap in price where no efficient two-sided trading occurred.

**Why:** Markets seek to achieve two-sided trade. When price moves too quickly, it leaves an imbalance the market statistically tends to return to fill.

**Trading FVGs:**
- Wait for price to retrace into the FVG
- Enter on confirmation of rejection at midpoint or lower boundary
- The midpoint (50%) is the highest-probability entry
- FVGs aligned with higher-TF bias and at structural levels have highest quality

---

### 3.3 Liquidity Pools

**Buy-side (BSL):** Above swing highs, equal highs, ATH, round numbers, prior session highs — long stop losses and breakout buys cluster here.

**Sell-side (SSL):** Below swing lows, equal lows, ATL, round numbers, prior session lows — short stop losses and breakout sells cluster here.

**Identifying high-quality liquidity pools:**
- Obvious, well-tested highs or lows
- Equal or near-equal swing points
- Prior highs/lows from significant timeframes
- Round number prices

**Trading strategy:** Wait for the sweep (stop hunt), then enter in the opposite direction once price closes back inside the range. Targets are the opposing liquidity pool.

---

### 3.4 Market Structure (BOS and CHoCH)

**BOS (Break of Structure):**
- Uptrend: closes above most recent swing high → trend continuation
- Downtrend: closes below most recent swing low → trend continuation
- Use BOS to stay aligned with trend, not as reversal signal

**CHoCH (Change of Character):**
- Uptrend: closes below most recent swing low → reversal warning
- Downtrend: closes above most recent swing high → reversal warning
- Must violate the swing created by the most recent BOS to be valid
- Alone insufficient — look for additional confirmation (FVG, OB, liquidity grab)

**Multi-timeframe use:** Higher TF BOS/CHoCH defines macro trend. Lower TF CHoCH confirms entry into a higher-TF OB or FVG.

---

### 3.5 Volume Profile (VAH, VAL, POC, HVN, LVN)

| Component | Definition | Trading Use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| POC (Point of Control) | Price level with highest traded volume | Magnetic level — acts as strong S/R |
| Value Area (VA) | Price range containing ~70% of all traded volume | Defines "accepted" / "fair" price zone |
| VAH (Value Area High) | Upper boundary of value area | Resistance from below; acceptance above = higher prices |
| VAL (Value Area Low) | Lower boundary of value area | Support from above; acceptance below = lower prices |
| HVN (High Volume Node) | Peak of volume at a specific price | Consolidation zone — price slows at HVNs |
| LVN (Low Volume Node) | Valley of low volume at a specific price | Fast transit zone — price moves rapidly through LVNs |

**High-Probability Setups:**

1. **Value Area Rotation (80% Rule):** Price opens outside value area and re-enters it → ~80% probability it traverses the entire VA to the opposite boundary.
2. **POC Return:** Price moves away from POC, returns to it. Enter on reaction confirmation.
3. **VAL Bounce:** Price retraces to prior session's VAL. Enter long on confirmation. Target: POC, then VAH.
4. **VAH Rejection:** Price tests VAH from below. Enter short on rejection. Target: POC, then VAL.
5. **LVN Breakout:** Price pushes through an LVN with volume confirmation. Moves through LVNs are fast.
6. **LVN Revisit (Carmine's Setup):** Price previously drove through an LVN, then returns. Enter on order flow confirmation.

**Key Principle:** Price gravitates toward HIGH volume (HVN, POC) and moves rapidly through LOW volume (LVN).

---

### 3.6 Footprint Charts — Delta, Bid/Ask Imbalances, Absorption, Stacked Imbalances

**What:** Displays volume traded at bid and ask for every price level within each candle — the internal battle between buyers and sellers.

**Delta:**
- Delta = Aggressive Buyers (volume at ask) minus Aggressive Sellers (volume at bid)
- Positive delta: buyers are aggressors; Negative delta: sellers are aggressors
- Divergences are the key signals (see below)

**Bid/Ask Imbalances:**
- Imbalance: one side has 3x or more volume than the other at the same price level
- Buying imbalance: Ask side 3x+ bid side → aggressive buyers dominating
- Selling imbalance: Bid side 3x+ ask side → aggressive sellers dominating
- Imbalances at key structural levels (POC, VAH, VAL, OB) are high-probability signals

**Stacked Imbalances:**
- Multiple imbalances of the same type in consecutive price levels within the same candle
- Stack of 3+ buying imbalances = intense, sustained institutional buying pressure
- Stacked imbalances mark strong S/R zones price will react to when revisited
- Bullish stacked imbalances on the way up become support on the pullback

**Absorption:**
- Significant volume trades at a specific price level but price does NOT move through it
- Indicates large limit orders defending the level — one of the highest-probability reversal signals
- Footprint appearance: large-volume cells at one price; candle may have long wick; big delta but minimal price movement

**Exhaustion:**
- Volume powering a move begins to decrease as price continues in the same direction
- Manifests as declining delta on successive candles making new highs/lows
- Precedes reversals but requires confirmation

**Delta Divergence:**
- **Bearish:** Price makes new high, delta makes lower high → buyers making new price with less conviction
- **Bullish:** Price makes new low, delta makes higher low → sellers losing force
- Confirm with CVD divergence, a volume profile level, or a price pattern

---

### 3.7 DOM (Depth of Market) / Order Book

**What:** Shows all pending limit orders resting at prices above and below current market price.

**Reading the DOM:**
- Large resting bid orders = potential support
- Large resting ask orders = potential resistance
- Watch whether large orders are **defended** (absorbing aggression, price reverses) or **pulled** (disappear before price arrives — spoofing)

**Tape Reading (Time and Sales):**
- Large prints executing at the ask = aggressive buying
- Large prints executing at the bid = aggressive selling
- Cluster of large prints at one price with no movement = absorption
- DOM shows intent; tape shows execution

**Advanced DOM Signals:**
- **Iceberg Orders:** Only a small portion visible; quantity refreshes as it fills. Appears on Bookmap as persistent refilling walls.
- **Spoofing:** Large orders appear, price moves toward them, then they disappear. Illegal but visible.
- **Stop-Run Trigger:** Price pushes through a prior high/low (where stops cluster), then immediately reverses.

---

### 3.8 Cumulative Delta (CVD)

**What:** Sums individual candle deltas over time, creating a running total showing whether buyers or sellers have dominated cumulatively.

**Reading CVD:**
- CVD rising + price rising = healthy bullish momentum
- CVD falling + price falling = healthy bearish momentum
- CVD rising + price falling = bullish divergence (hidden strength)
- CVD falling + price rising = bearish divergence (hidden weakness) → REVERSAL SIGNAL

**High-Probability Divergences:**
- **Bearish:** Price makes higher highs; CVD makes lower highs → buyer conviction weakening
- **Bullish:** Price makes lower lows; CVD makes higher lows → seller conviction weakening

**Best practice:** Never use CVD divergence as standalone signal. Combine with: volume profile level + structural level (OB, FVG, liquidity pool) + price action confirmation.

---

### 3.9 Absorption vs. Exhaustion

| Feature | Absorption | Exhaustion |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Volume | Very high at a specific level | Declining over the course of a move |
| Price movement | Minimal despite high volume | Continues but slows/stalls |
| What is happening | Passive limit orders stopping aggressive flow | Aggressive orders losing momentum |
| Typical location | Key structural levels (S/R, POC, OB) | New highs/lows after extended moves |
| Signal type | Reversal (from the absorbing side) | Momentum fading; reversal possible |
| Footprint | High-volume cells at one price; candle closes against the flow | Delta shrinking on each new candle; divergence |

---

### 3.10 Stop Hunts / Liquidity Grabs

**Mechanics:** Institutions need large volumes. Retail stop losses (which become market orders when triggered) provide the liquidity institutions need.

**Identifying a Stop Hunt:**
- Sharp, fast move through a well-established high or low (often a wick)
- Triggers obvious stop levels (swing highs/lows, round numbers, prior session extremes)
- Price immediately reverses — the hunt was the purpose, not the direction
- Volume spikes during the sweep
- Price closes back inside the prior range within the same or very next candle

**Trading Around Stop Hunts:**
1. Mark all obvious liquidity pools
2. Do NOT place stops at obvious levels
3. Wait for the sweep to occur and price to close back inside the range
4. Enter OPPOSITE to the sweep direction
5. Stop beyond the extreme of the sweep wick
6. Target: the opposing liquidity pool

**Best combination:** Stop hunt sweeping a liquidity pool + price returns into unmitigated OB or FVG + CHoCH on 5-minute chart = very high probability entry

---

### 3.11 Premium and Discount Zones

**Application:**
1. Identify the current dealing range (significant swing high to swing low)
2. Draw Fibonacci from swing low to swing high
3. 50% = equilibrium
4. 0.618, 0.705, 0.79 = discount zone (OTE for longs)
5. 0.382, 0.25, 0.0 (approaching swing high) = premium for shorts

**Key Rule:** Never long in premium zone; never short in discount zone.

---

### 3.12 Session Highs/Lows (London, New York, Asia)

| Session | EST Time | Character |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Asian | ~20:00–00:00 | Accumulation. Marks the Asian Range (ASH/ASL) for London to manipulate. |
| London | ~02:00–11:00 | Sweeps the Asian high or low (manipulation), then starts the true daily move. |
| New York | ~07:00–16:00 | Confirms or continues London direction. NY open (08:30–11:00) is highest volatility. |

**Session Strategy:** Asia builds the trap → London triggers it (sweeps the Asian range) → NY exploits the resulting trend.

**High-probability play:** Asian consolidation → London sweeps ASL (grabs sell-side liquidity) → price reverses bullish → enter long on NY open confirmation.

### 3.13 Opening Range / Initial Balance

**Opening Range (OR):**
- Price range created in the first 5–15 minutes of the session
- ORH and ORL are key reference levels for the day
- Break above ORH with volume = bullish bias; break below ORL = bearish bias

**Initial Balance (IB) — Market Profile:**
- Price range formed during the first 60 minutes
- IB high and low define the day's character
- Stays within IB all day = narrow, balanced day (fade the extremes)
- Extends beyond IB by 2x+ = trend day (trade aggressively in direction)

**Day Types:**
1. **Normal Day:** 85%+ within IB. Fade extremes.
2. **Trend Day:** Breaks out of IB and continues. Add to winners.
3. **Neutral Day:** Extends beyond IB on both sides. Choppy — reduce size.
4. **Double-Distribution Day:** Two distinct value areas form.

---

### 3.14 VWAP and Anchored VWAP (AVWAP)

**Standard VWAP:**
- Volume-Weighted Average Price from session open
- Institutional desks benchmark against VWAP — they buy below, sell above
- Price above VWAP = buyers in control; price below = sellers in control
- Clean reclaim of VWAP after being below = bullish; loss of VWAP after being above = bearish

**Key Anchor Points for AVWAP:**
- Prior swing highs and swing lows
- FOMC announcements, CPI prints, earnings releases
- Weekly open, monthly open, quarterly open
- Breakout candle
- Annual/quarterly open (macro analysis)

**Multiple AVWAP Confluence:** When two or more AVWAPs from different significant points converge near the same price = high-probability S/R zone.

---

### 3.15 Time and Price (TPO / Market Profile)

**What:** Assigns a letter (TPO = Time Price Opportunity) to each 30-minute period at every price level visited. Shows WHERE price spent the most time.

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| Single Print | Only one TPO letter at a price level — price moved so fast only 30 min spent there. LVNs. Often filled in later sessions. |
| TPO Count | Number of 30-min periods at a given price. High = accepted; low = transit. |
| POC | Price level with most TPO letters — most time spent. |
| Value Area | 70% of TPO letters surrounding the POC. |
| Open-Drive | Opens and immediately drives one direction. Strongest trend signal. |
| Open-Test-Drive | Tests a prior level, then drives in opposite direction. |
| Open-Rejection-Reverse | Tries to go one way, gets rejected, reverses strongly. |
| Open-Auction | Quiet, two-way auction within prior value area. Balanced/choppy day. |

## PART 4: CONFLUENCE-BASED ENTRY FRAMEWORK

### The Three-Layer Framework

**Layer 1 — Context (Higher Timeframe):**
- What is the daily/weekly trend direction?
- Are we in premium or discount relative to the weekly/monthly range?
- What are the next major liquidity targets above and below?
- Which side (buyers or sellers) has the structural advantage?

**Layer 2 — Location (The Level of Interest):**
- Unmitigated order block aligned with higher-TF bias
- Fair value gap from a significant displacement move
- Volume profile level (POC, VAH, VAL from prior session, weekly, or developing)
- Liquidity pool about to be or just swept
- Session-based level (prior day high/low, Asian range high/low)
- Significant VWAP or Anchored VWAP

**Layer 3 — Confirmation (Real-Time Order Flow):**
- Delta shift from negative to positive (for longs) at the level
- Absorption visible on the footprint — big volume, price not breaking
- CVD making higher lows while price makes lower lows (bullish divergence)
- DOM showing large bids defended and refilled
- CHoCH on the 5-minute or 1-minute chart
- Rejection wick or engulfing candle on the 15-minute chart
- Stop hunt below the level followed by immediate reversal

---

### High-Probability Setup Scoring (3+ out of 5 = valid trade)

| Condition | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Higher-TF trend aligned with trade direction | High |
| Price in premium/discount zone appropriate for trade | High |
| Price at a significant, unmitigated OB or FVG | High |
| Order flow confirmation at the level (absorption, delta flip, CHoCH) | High |
| Preceding liquidity grab (stop hunt) before entry | High |
| Volume profile level confluence (POC, VAH, VAL, LVN) | Medium |
| Session-based timing (kill zone, London/NY open) | Medium |
| VWAP/AVWAP confluence at entry level | Medium |
| CVD divergence confirming the setup | Medium |
| Multi-timeframe agreement (daily, 4H, 1H same bias) | High |

- **A+ Setup (5+ conditions):** Size maximally — these are the "pocket aces" trades
- **B Setup (3–4 conditions):** Standard sizing
- **C Setup (1–2 conditions):** Do not take — insufficient edge

---

### Red Flags (Disqualify or Reduce Conviction)

- Trading against higher-TF trend without CHoCH confirmation
- Price in premium zone AND setup is long, or discount zone AND setup is short
- OB or FVG already touched once (mitigated setups have lower probability)
- No order flow confirmation — relying solely on price levels
- Low volume, lunch hour, or pre-market timing
- Major economic release within 15 minutes
- Rangebound/choppy market with no clear structure
- Multiple competing levels at the entry zone

## PART 5: TRADE MANAGEMENT

### Stop Loss Placement

**Universal Rule:** The stop goes at the point of structural invalidation — not an arbitrary distance.

| Setup Type | Stop Placement |
|-----------|---------------|
| Order Block entry | Just beyond the opposite extreme of the OB |
| FVG entry | Just beyond the outer boundary of the FVG |
| Swing Failure Pattern | Just beyond the extreme wick of the sweep |
| LVN entry | Just beyond the LVN zone, beyond the recent swing high/low |
| Absorption entry | Just beyond the absorption zone |
| Capitulation fade | Just beyond the extreme wick of the capitulation candle |
| ICT OTE entry | Beyond the swing low/high defining the dealing range |

**Position Sizing Formula:**
```
Position Size = (Account Risk $ per Trade) / (Entry Price − Stop Price × Point Value)
```
1% risk rule: no more than 1% of account equity per trade. Professional traders use 0.5% for average and scale to 1–2% for A+ setups.

---

### Profit Target Placement

**Target 1 (50% of position, 1:1 to 1.5:1 R:R):**
- Nearest significant structure level in the target direction
- VWAP or daily POC
- 50% of the target FVG midpoint

**Target 2 (remaining 50%, or 25% after T1, 2:1 to 3:1 R:R):**
- Opposing session high/low
- Next unmitigated OB or FVG in target direction
- Major volume profile level (prior session POC, weekly POC)
- Opposing liquidity pool

**Target 3 (final 25%, 3:1 to 5:1+ R:R, swing trades):**
- Weekly or monthly structure level
- ATH or ATL
- Measured move target

---

### Trailing Stops and Trade Management

**After Target 1:** Move stop to breakeven — creates a free trade.

**ATR-Based Trailing:** 2x–3x ATR below current price. Keeps trade alive through normal volatility.

**Structure-Based Trailing:** After each new swing high (for a long), move stop to just below the most recent higher low. Exit when lower-TF CHoCH fires.

**Kill the Trade Immediately If:**
- Order flow turns against you on the tape
- CVD flips and does not recover
- Major unexpected news event
- Price closes below the key structural level you entered from

---

### Partial Exit Philosophy

- Scale out in portions (50%/25%/25% or 33%/33%/33%) at predefined structural levels
- Move stop to breakeven after first partial — transforms all subsequent partials into pure profit
- Never exit 100% at a single level unless there is strong reason (e.g., strong absorption at target)

## PART 8: THE WYCKOFF METHOD — FOUNDATIONAL CONTEXT

### The Four Wyckoff Phases

**Accumulation (Institutional Buying — after sustained downtrend):**
- Composite Man quietly absorbs supply from panicking sellers
- Key events: Selling Climax (SC), Automatic Rally (AR), Secondary Test (ST)
- **Spring:** Price dips below range support (stops triggered), immediately reclaims above it. Final liquidity grab before mark-up. Highest-probability long entry.

**Mark-Up (Trend Higher):**
- Volume increases on rallies; low volume on pullbacks
- Pullbacks to prior resistance-turned-support with order flow confirmation = entries

**Distribution (Institutional Selling — after sustained uptrend):**
- Composite Man distributes into strength, absorbing demand from retail
- **Upthrust:** Price spikes above distribution range resistance (triggers breakout buyers), then immediately reverses. Mirror image of Spring. High-probability short.

**Mark-Down (Trend Lower):**
- Rallies to resistance with weak volume and selling absorption = entries

**Connecting Wyckoff to Modern Order Flow:**
- Spring = ICT Stop Hunt below SSL + reverse → bullish OB/FVG entry
- Upthrust = ICT Stop Hunt above BSL + reverse → bearish OB/FVG entry
- Accumulation range = ICT AMD Accumulation (Asian session equivalent)
- SC/BC = Capitulation candles (Lance Breitstein's setup)

## PART 9: INTEGRATED HIGH-PROBABILITY TRADING PROCESS

### Pre-Session Preparation

1. Mark prior session levels: prior day high/low, POC, VAH, VAL
2. Mark weekly levels: weekly high/low/open/POC
3. Identify unmitigated OBs and FVGs from daily and 4-hour charts
4. Identify next major liquidity pools (where BSL and SSL rests)
5. Note premium/discount status relative to weekly range
6. Check economic calendar — mark all high-impact releases; avoid entries 15 min before/after
7. Define session bias (bullish or bearish based on daily chart)

### During Session — Finding the Setup

1. Monitor session opens: London (02:00–05:00 EST) and NY (07:00–10:00 EST) for liquidity grabs
2. Watch for Asia range sweep — does London sweep ASH or ASL? Note direction, prepare for reversal
3. Wait for price to arrive at pre-marked level (POC, OB, FVG, LVN, session high/low, VWAP)
4. Switch to 15-minute or 5-minute chart — begin monitoring order flow at the level
5. Check footprint: absorption occurring? Delta diverging? Stacked imbalances forming?
6. Check DOM: large bids or asks appearing and holding?
7. Check CVD: divergence between price and cumulative delta?
8. Wait for CHoCH on 5-minute or 15-minute chart

### Entry Execution

1. CHoCH fires on lower TF AND footprint/delta confirm → enter
2. Market order (decisive confirmation) or limit order (slightly inside OB/FVG for better price)
3. Set stop at structural invalidation point immediately
4. Set Target 1 at next logical structure level
5. Set Target 2 at opposing liquidity pool or daily high/low

### Trade Management

1. At Target 1: exit 50%, move stop to breakeven
2. At Target 2: exit 25%, trail remaining stop using structure or ATR
3. Final 25%: let run with trailing stop; kill on any reversal signal in order flow
4. If order flow turns against trade → exit immediately

## PART 10: COMMON MISTAKES

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|---------|
| Trading every FVG/OB without context | Always check premium/discount, higher TF bias, and order flow confirmation |
| Placing stops at obvious levels | Place at structural invalidation; accept full risk or skip the trade |
| Ignoring the higher timeframe | Start every analysis from weekly/daily — always top-down |
| Revenge trading after a loss | Hard daily loss limit (3–5% of account); stop trading when hit |
| Overcomplicating the workspace | Master one or two tools at a time; remove anything not actively used |
| Trading during low-liquidity sessions | Only trade during kill zones and high-liquidity windows |
| Relying only on price levels without order flow | Order flow is the confirmation layer; levels without confirmation are guesses |
| Taking a mitigated OB or FVG | Track which levels have been visited; unmitigated = fresh; mitigated = expired |
| Over-leveraging | Fix risk in dollar terms; not percentage of leverage |
